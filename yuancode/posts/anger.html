tell me where to put the anger

A blog by Juan Carlo Sibalon

Anger is a powerful emotion, a force that can be both destructive and illuminating. It bubbles up from deep within, a boiling pot of frustration and hurt, often spilling over in ways I don’t intend. But where does it go? What do I do with this energy that feels so potent yet so confusing? It’s as if I’m holding a lit match, wondering where to strike without causing a wildfire.

In moments of anger, I grapple with the instinct to lash out or retreat. Sometimes, it feels like I’m standing on the edge of a precipice, teetering between the urge to express my feelings and the fear of the consequences. Will my words cause pain? Will they create a rift that can’t be mended? I find myself caught in a cycle of internal debate, wrestling with the desire to be heard while also wanting to shield others from my storms.

Yet, I know that ignoring my anger is not the answer. It lingers, a shadow haunting my thoughts, reminding me of what I’ve yet to confront. Instead of letting it fester, perhaps the real challenge is to channel this energy into something constructive. Can I transform my anger into action? Can I use it to fuel my passion for change, to fight for what I believe in, rather than allowing it to consume me?

I yearn to understand my anger—to dissect it, to learn from it, and ultimately to let it guide me towards growth. Each flare-up is an invitation to explore the root causes of my frustrations. It’s a call to acknowledge my feelings without judgment and to seek healthy outlets for expression. Maybe it’s through writing, art, or conversations that I can begin to untangle this complex web of emotions.

So, tell me where to put the anger. Should I let it ignite a fire within me that drives positive change, or should I channel it into reflection and understanding? I hope to find a balance, to honor my emotions while ensuring they don’t dictate my actions. Anger can be a catalyst for growth if I embrace it wisely, a reminder that even in moments of turmoil, there is an opportunity for healing and transformation.
