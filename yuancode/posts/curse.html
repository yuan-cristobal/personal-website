the curse of a forgotten dream

A blog by Juan Carlo Sibalon

There once was a time when my dreams felt limitless, vibrant with possibilities, and fueled by unyielding ambition. Each aspiration flickered like a star, illuminating the night sky of my mind with visions of what could be. I remember the excitement of sketching grand plans, crafting a future that seemed just within reach. But as life unfolded its complex tapestry, many of those dreams began to fade into the background, forgotten in the hustle and bustle of daily responsibilities and the relentless march of time.

Reflecting on these lost aspirations, I can’t help but feel a pang of regret. It’s as if I’ve cursed myself by letting them slip away, one by one, until they became distant echoes in my memory. The dream of traveling to far-off places, of writing stories that resonate with others, or of pursuing passions that once ignited my spirit—these visions are now ghosts of who I once was. Instead of nurturing them, I buried them beneath layers of practicality and the demands of adulthood, convincing myself they were unrealistic or too frivolous for my current reality.

Perhaps the true curse lies not in the dreams themselves but in my acceptance of their abandonment. The weight of unfulfilled potential hangs heavy on my heart, reminding me of the vibrant life I once envisioned. Each day, moments spark flickers of inspiration, yet they are often snuffed out by the practicality of my routine. I’ve become a spectator in my own life, watching as the dreams that once danced in my mind quietly slip away.

Yet, as I ponder the nature of these forgotten dreams, I realize they are not entirely lost. They linger in the corners of my heart, whispering for attention, urging me to reclaim what I once held dear. Perhaps it’s never too late to breathe life back into them, to dust off those aspirations, and take steps—no matter how small—toward making them a reality. The curse of a forgotten dream is not an irreversible fate but a challenge—a call to reconnect with my true self and rekindle that lost spark.

In this journey of rediscovery, I hope to embrace the beauty of chasing dreams again, allowing myself to dream boldly and without reservation. It’s time to honor the vision I once had for my life and confront the fears that kept me from pursuing it. I am reminded that every day is an opportunity to reclaim those aspirations and turn them into something tangible. The curse can become a blessing if I dare to dream again, to believe in the power of possibility, and to redefine my path with courage and conviction.
