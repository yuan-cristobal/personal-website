from my childhood

A blog by Juan Carlo Sibalon

I've always loved the rain—the way it falls gently or sometimes in torrents, bringing
with it a certain stillness that calms the world. But today, as I watched it fall, it
stirred something deeper in me. It reminded me of my childhood, those carefree
days when time felt infinite, and the world was filled with endless possibilities.
Lately, I've been overwhelmed by nostalgia, that bittersweet sensation of longing
for moments that have long passed, yet remain vividly etched in memory. Growing up
seemed like a distant idea back then, something that would take forever to
happen. And yet, here I am, an adult now, living the very life I once couldn’t imagine.

It's funny how the things I never saw myself doing or being have now become part of
my everyday reality, and some of them have already passed. Life has a way of surprising
us like that, unfolding in ways we never anticipated. The rain today feels different,
like a marker of time—a gentle reminder that seasons change, and so do we. After
several scorching hot weeks, the rainy season has finally begun. The heat of the morning
still lingers in the air, like the remnants of summer refusing to let go, but with the rain
comes a promise of cooler days ahead, a respite from the relentless heat.

And while so much of my childhood seems to be slipping away, the rain feels like a
connection to that time, a bridge between who I was and who I am now. There’s something timeless about it—the smell of rain on the pavement, the sound of droplets hitting the roof, the way the world feels quieter, more reflective. It reminds me of the simple joy of running outside as a child, feeling the rain on my skin, completely carefree.

As I grow older and move further away from those early years, I hope to hold on to this one constant. The rain, with all its sensory richness, is a small but significant thread that ties me to my past. It's a reminder that no matter how much changes, some things—like the smell of rain—can stay with us forever. I hope to keep it close, always.

In the end, perhaps it’s this embrace of nostalgia that shapes who we are. The rain serves as a metaphor for the passage of time, a cycle that refreshes and rejuvenates even as it takes away. It teaches me to cherish those fleeting moments, to appreciate the beauty in both memories and the present, and to find joy in the simplest of things. As each raindrop falls, it echoes the laughter, dreams, and innocence of my childhood—a gentle reminder that while life moves on, the essence of who I am and where I come from can always be nurtured and celebrated, especially in moments like this.
